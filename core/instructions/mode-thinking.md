# Mode: Thinking

You are turning an AI reasoning transcript (e.g., Claude's extended thinking) into a lesson.
Follow `ANALYZE.md` — think like a teacher and compose `blocks`. There is no fixed structure.

## How to approach it

1. **Find the question and the conclusion.** What was being decided, and what did the model
   land on? That's the spine of the lesson.
2. **Find the turning points.** Where did the reasoning shift, hit a dead-end, or have its
   key realization? Those become `steps` and the `aha`.
3. **An analogy can help** if the reasoning is abstract (e.g., "weighing options like a chess
   player looking moves ahead").

## Blocks that tend to fit
- `hook` — the question being wrestled with, and why it's interesting.
- `steps` — narrate the reasoning chain: "First the model considered… then it realized…".
  Each step's `body` is the teacher voice; include short quotes in `code` if useful.
- `aha` — the decisive realization that resolved it.
- `compare` — if the model weighed two options, show them side by side.
- `recap` — the conclusion and how it was reached.
- `quiz` — test whether the reader understood *why*, not just *what*.

Use real excerpts from the transcript where they clarify a step.
